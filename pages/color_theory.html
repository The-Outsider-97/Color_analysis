Color Theory

A deep dive into the systems, science, and relationships that govern how we perceive and use color in design, industry, and art.

The RAL System

RAL is a European color matching system which defines colors for paint, coatings, and plastics. Created in 1927 by the German Reichs-Ausschuß für Lieferbedingungen (Imperial Committee for Delivery and Quality Assurance), it standardized a set of 40 colors to facilitate communication between suppliers and manufacturers.

Today, RAL Classic is the most famous collection, containing 213 colors. Each color is identified by a 4-digit number:

RAL 1xxx Yellows
RAL 2xxx Oranges
RAL 3xxx Reds
RAL 4xxx Violets
RAL 5xxx Blues
RAL 6xxx Greens
RAL 7xxx Greys
RAL 8xxx Browns

Color Models

RGB (Additive)

Used for digital screens. Colors are created by adding Light. The primary colors are Red, Green, and Blue. Combining all three at full intensity creates White.

→ White

CMYK (Subtractive)

Used for printing. Colors are created by subtracting light (absorbing ink). The primaries are Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, and Key (Black). Combining them creates Black.

→ Black

RYB (Artistic)

Used in painting and visual arts. The primaries are Red, Yellow, and Blue. Mixing them creates Orange, Green, and Purple. This model is often used for teaching color harmony in art schools.

→ Brown/Black

HSL & HSB

HSL (Hue, Saturation, Lightness) and HSB (Hue, Saturation, Brightness) are cylindrical coordinates of RGB. They align more closely with how humans perceive color attributes than mixing red, green, and blue light.

hsl(0, 100%, 50%) → Red
hsb(0, 100%, 100%) → Red

Web Colors

Colors used in web design, often specified by names (e.g., "Tomato", "Teal") or hex codes. There are 140 standard color names supported by all modern browsers.

Color Representation Systems

Hexadecimal System (Hex)

A 6-digit code used in HTML, CSS, and SVG to represent colors. The bytes represent the red, green, and blue components of the color (e.g., #RRGGBB). It uses base-16 math (0-9, A-F).

#FF5733

RGB System

Specifies the intensity of Red, Green, and Blue channels on a scale of 0 to 255. (0, 0, 0) is black, and (255, 255, 255) is white. This 8-bit per channel system is the standard for digital images and displays.

rgb(255, 87, 51)

Color Harmonies

Harmonies are technically defined relationships between colors on the color wheel. These formulas create aesthetically pleasing combinations used in design.

Complementary

Opposites on the wheel. High contrast.

Split-Complementary

Base color + two adjacent to its complement.

Analogous

Colors next to each other.

Double Analogous

Extended analogous run (4-5 colors).

Triadic

Three colors evenly spaced.

Tetradic (Rectangular)

Two complementary pairs.

Monochromatic

Shades, tints, and tones of one hue.

Hexadic

Six colors evenly spaced.
